51 /* Calculate the melting temperature of substr(seq, start, length) using the
52  formula from Bolton and McCarthy, PNAS 84:1390 (1962) as presented in
53  Sambrook, Fritsch and Maniatis, Molecular Cloning, p 11.46 (1989, CSHL
54  Press).
55 
56  Tm = 81.5 + 16.6(log10([Na+])) + .41*(%GC) - 600/length
57 
58  Where [Na+] is the molar sodium concentration, (%GC) is the percent of Gs
59  and Cs in the sequence, and length is the length of the sequence.
60 
61  A similar formula is used by the prime primer selection program in GCG
62  (http://www.gcg.com), which instead uses 675.0 / length in the last term
63  (after F. Baldino, Jr, M.-F. Chesselet, and M.E. Lewis, Methods in
64  Enzymology 168:766 (1989) eqn (1) on page 766 without the mismatch and
65  formamide terms). The formulas here and in Baldino et al. assume Na+ rather
66  than K+. According to J.G. Wetmur, Critical Reviews in BioChem. and
67  Mol. Bio. 26:227 (1991) 50 mM K+ should be equivalent in these formulae to .2
68  M Na+.
69 
70  This function takes salt_conc to be the millimolar (mM) concentration,
71  since mM is the usual units in PCR applications.
72 
73  */
74 double long_seq_tm(const char *seq,
75  int start,
76  int length,
77  double salt_conc,
78  double divalent_conc,
79  double dntp_conc);

81 /*
82  For olgigotm() and seqtm()
83 
84  Both functions return the melting temperature of the given oligo
85  calculated as specified by user, but oligotm _should_ only be used on
86  DNA sequences of length <= MAX_PRIMER_LENGTH (which is defined
87  elsewhere). seqtm uses oligotm for sequences of length <=
88  MAX_PRIMER_LENGTH, and a different, G+C% based formula for longer
89  sequences. For oligotm(), no error is generated on sequences
90  longer than MAX_PRIMER_LENGTH, but the formula becomes less
91  accurate as the sequence grows longer. Caveat emptor.
92 
93  We use the folowing typedefs:
94 */

106 /*
107  If tm_method==santalucia_auto, then the table of
108  nearest-neighbor thermodynamic parameters and method for Tm
109  calculation in the paper [SantaLucia JR (1998) "A unified view of
110  polymer, dumbbell and oligonucleotide DNA nearest-neighbor
111  thermodynamics", Proc Natl Acad Sci 95:1460-65
112  http://dx.doi.org/10.1073/pnas.95.4.1460] is used.
113  *THIS IS THE RECOMMENDED VALUE*.
114  Added by T. Koressaar
115 
116  If tm_method==breslauer_auto, then method for Tm
117  calculations in the paper [Rychlik W, Spencer WJ and Rhoads RE
118  (1990) "Optimization of the annealing temperature for DNA
119  amplification in vitro", Nucleic Acids Res 18:6409-12
120  http://www.pubmedcentral.nih.gov/articlerender.fcgi?tool=pubmed&pubmedid=2243783].
121  and the thermodynamic parameters in the paper [Breslauer KJ, Frank
122  R, Blöcker H and Marky LA (1986) "Predicting DNA duplex stability
123  from the base sequence" Proc Natl Acad Sci 83:4746-50
124  http://dx.doi.org/10.1073/pnas.83.11.3746], are is used. This is
125  the method and the table that primer3 used up to and including
126  version 1.0.1
127 
128  If salt_corrections==schildkraut, then formula for
129  salt correction in the paper [Schildkraut, C, and Lifson, S (1965)
130  "Dependence of the melting temperature of DNA on salt
131  concentration", Biopolymers 3:195-208 (not available on-line)] is
132  used. This is the formula that primer3 used up to and including
133  version 1.0.1.
134 
135  If salt_corrections==santalucia, then formula for
136  salt correction suggested by the paper [SantaLucia JR (1998) "A
137  unified view of polymer, dumbbell and oligonucleotide DNA
138  nearest-neighbor thermodynamics", Proc Natl Acad Sci 95:1460-65
139  http://dx.doi.org/10.1073/pnas.95.4.1460] is used.
140 
141  *THIS IS THE RECOMMENDED VALUE*.
142  Added by T.Koressaar
143 
144  If salt_corrections==owczarzy, then formula for
145  salt correction in the paper [Owczarzy R, You Y, Moreira BG,
146  Manthey JA, Huang L, Behlke MA and Walder JA (2004) "Effects of
147  sodium ions on DNA duplex oligomers: Improved predictions of
148  melting temperatures", Biochemistry 43:3537-54
149  http://dx.doi.org/10.1021/bi034621r] is used.
150  Added by T.Koressaar
